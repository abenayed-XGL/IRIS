# {% include icon.html icon="fa-solid fa-microscope" %}Research

Research at the **RF Systems Integration (RF-SI) Laboratory** focuses on the development of next-generation RF transceiver systems through the tight integration of **hardware, signal processing, and measurement science**. The laboratory adopts a system-level perspective to address the challenges of achieving **efficient, high-quality signal generation and transmission** at mm-wave and sub-THz frequencies.

In particular, the RF-SI Laboratory works on **novel transceiver architectures** that enable wideband and high-fidelity signal generation; the design of **advanced beamforming antenna arrays** that account for hardware non-idealities and array-level effects; the development of **large-scale MIMO transmitters** through co-optimization of circuits, arrays, and signal processing; and **digital signal processing methods** aimed at optimizing transceiver performance through calibration, beamforming, and linearization.

A core pillar of the laboratory’s research is **high-frequency measurement science**, which provides the experimental foundation needed to bridge the gap between theoretical models, simulations, and real-world system behavior. Through measurement-driven design and validation, the RF-SI Laboratory ensures that advances in RF circuits, antenna arrays, and signal processing translate into practical, deployable systems.

This research is motivated by applications in **next-generation wireless communications, satellite systems, and high-frequency sensing**, with particular relevance to **6G and beyond**.
